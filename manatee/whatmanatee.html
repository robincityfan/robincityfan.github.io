What Are Manatees?

Manatees are one of the two types of sirenians left on earth.

This is the list of manatees alive today:

  • The Amazonian Manatees
  • The West Indian Manatee
  • The African Manatee
  • The Dwarf Manatee*
  • The Florida Manatee

*not reconized by the IUCN

Manatees are related to hyraxes and elaphants.

Manatees weigh roughly 500 grams and are normaly 3 metre long.

Manatees have beaver-like tails and can dive for up to 15 minutes.

They can swim 5 to 10 kp/h.
